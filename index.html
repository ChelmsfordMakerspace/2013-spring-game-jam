The Challenge

Over the course of a weekend, individuals or teams build a will be challenged to produce a working game or protype. You can use any framework, engine or hardware that you like.

The Rules

Within reason, you can use any language, game engine or framework you like to build your prototype, you can also built it for any platform or input method (Kinect, Playstation Move, Wii Remote, Balance Board, etc) you like as long as you can demonstrate it.

As well as the standard "Best in show" awards, judges will also be looking to award strange and new ideas in made up categories, some suggested ones are listed below

  • Accessible gaming
  • Different Input Methods
  • Hacking existing games

What do I bring?

  • Toolkits, engines and software you plan to use

What do I have to know beforehand?

We would recommend at least some previous experience in something, we're open to anything from Scratch right down to raw Assembly, including engines such as Unity, UDK or Source. HTML, CSS and Javascript would be useful for web based games. You won't be judged on what you've used to create your project but how you have used it and your project itself.

Do I have to make a game?

Nope! If it's interactive and entertaining, uses consumer gaming hardware or is in some way related, it's allowed. We encourage making something different and crazy, remember we're the kind of people who fly AR.Drones with bananas!

Food

While you're at the Game Jam you'll get lunch and diner, the likelihood is we'll go on a food run getting pizza, fish and chips, chinese etc. but this is dependent on sponsors and what people want. We will also be providing snacks and drinks throughout the day. If you have special dietary requirements, please tell us beforehand so we can accomodate for you.

The Semi-legal stuff

  • Attendees keep all intellectual property they create.
  • To be judged, all projects must be publicly shown at the end of the jam and be listed on the site.
  • While it is not in any way required, we would prefer projects to have a downloadable demo or be open-sourced.
