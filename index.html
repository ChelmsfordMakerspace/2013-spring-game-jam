What is Chelmsford Game Jam?

Over the course of a weekend, individuals or teams build a small game or prototype. At the end, we hope that they are able present their creations to everyone else and talk about how they made it.

Guidelines

Within reason, you can use any language, game engine or framework you like to build your prototype, you can also built it for any platform or input method (Kinect, Playstation Move, Wii Remote, Balance Board, etc) you like as long as you can demonstrate it.

As well as the standard "Best in show" awards, judges will also be looking to award strange and new ideas in made up categories, some suggested ones are listed below

  • Accessable gaming
  • Different Input Methods

What do I have to know?

We would recommend at least some previous experience in something, we're open to anything from Scratch right up to raw Assembly. You won't be judged on what you've used to create your project but how you have used it to build your project.

Semi-legal stuff

  • Attendees keep all intellectual property they create.
  • To be judged, all projects must be publicly shown at the end of the jam and be listed on the site.
  • While it is not in any way required, we would prefer projects to have a downloadable demo or be open-sourced.

Food

While you're at the Game Jam you'll get free lunch and diner, the likelihood is we'll go on a food run getting pizza, fish and chips, chinese etc. but this is dependent on sponsors. We'll also be providing snacks and drinks throughout the day. If you have special dietary requirements, please tell us beforehand so we can accomodate for you.
